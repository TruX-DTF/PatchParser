               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <h1><a name="UPD%3ASpecification"></a>UPD: Specification</h1> 
                  <p>To cap facet counts by 1 specify <tt>facet.exists=true</tt>. It can be used with <tt>facet.method=enum</tt> or when it's omitted. It can be used only on non-trie fields i.e. strings. It may speed up facet counting on large indices and/or high-cardinality facet values.. </p> 
                  <h3><a name="Shotversion%3A"></a>Shot version:</h3> 
                  <p>This improves performance for facet.method=enum when it's enough to know that facet count&gt;0, for example when you it's when you dynamically populate filters on search form. New method checks if two bitsets intersect instead of counting intersection size.</p> 
                  <h3><a name="Longversion%3A"></a>Long version:</h3> 
                  <p>We have a dataset containing hundreds of millions of records, we facet by dozens of fields with many of facet-excludes and have relatively small number of unique values in fields, around thousands.<br> Before executing search, users work with "advanced search" form, our goal is to populate dozens of filters with values which are applicable with other selected values, so basically this is a use case for facets with mincount=1, but without need in actual counts.</p> 
                  <p>Our performance tests showed that facet.method=enum works much better than fc\fcs, probably due to a specific ratio of "docset"\"unique terms count". For example average execution of query time with method fc=1500ms, fcs=2600ms and with enum=280ms. Profiling indicated the majority time for enum was spent on intersecting docsets.</p> 
                  <p>Hers's a patch that introduces an extension to facet calculation for method=enum. Basically it uses docSetA.intersects(docSetB) instead of docSetA. intersectionSize (docSetB).</p> 
                  <p>As a result we were able to reduce our average query time from 280ms to 60ms.</p>
