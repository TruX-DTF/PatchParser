                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>The DatabaseMetaData method supportsResultSetConcurrency erroneously<br> returns false on the network client for all arguments combination, cf<br> the attached repro program. The embedded client returns correct<br> results, viz the output:</p> 
                  <p>org.apache.derby.jdbc.ClientDriver:<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_FORWARD_ONLY,CONCUR_READ_ONLY: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_FORWARD_ONLY,CONCUR_UPDATABLE: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_INSENSITIVE,CONCUR_READ_ONLY: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_INSENSITIVE,CONCUR_UPDATABLE: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_SENSITIVE,CONCUR_READ_ONLY: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_SENSITIVE,CONCUR_UPDATABLE: false<br> org.apache.derby.jdbc.EmbeddedDriver:<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_FORWARD_ONLY,CONCUR_READ_ONLY: true<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_FORWARD_ONLY,CONCUR_UPDATABLE: true<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_INSENSITIVE,CONCUR_READ_ONLY: true<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_INSENSITIVE,CONCUR_UPDATABLE: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_SENSITIVE,CONCUR_READ_ONLY: false<br> SupportsResultSetConcurrency: TYPE_SCROLL_SENSITIVE,CONCUR_UPDATABLE: false</p> 
                  <p>Presumably, this is wrong in released versions as well.</p> 
                 </div> 
                </div>
