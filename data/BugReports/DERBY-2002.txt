                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>According to the SQL:2003 spec, section 6.11 &lt;case expression&gt; Syntax Rule 3. At least one &lt;result&gt; in a <br> &lt;case specification&gt; shall specify a &lt;result expression&gt;. Derby currently is violating this rule. e.g.:</p> 
                  <p>ij&gt; values case when 1=2 then NULL when 1=3 then NULL else NULL end;<br> 1</p> 
                  <hr> 
                  <p>NULL</p> 
                  <p>1 row selected</p> 
                  <p>6.11 &lt;case expression&gt; Format section defines &lt;result&gt; as the following:</p> 
                  <p>&lt;result&gt; ::= <br> &lt;result expression&gt;</p> 
                  <div class="table-wrap"> 
                   <table class="confluenceTable">
                    <tbody> 
                     <tr> 
                      <td class="confluenceTd"> NULL</td> 
                     </tr> 
                    </tbody>
                   </table> 
                  </div> 
                  <p>The above statement should have thrown a SQLException instead of returning a result.</p>
