                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>

               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>5 years ago (<a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/LUCENE-1458" title="Further steps towards flexible indexing" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="LUCENE-1458"><del>LUCENE-1458</del></a>) we "enabled" fully binary terms in the<br> lowest levels of Lucene (the codec APIs) yet today, actually adding an<br> arbitrary byte[] binary term during indexing is far from simple: you<br> must make a custom Field with a custom TokenStream and a custom<br> TermToBytesRefAttribute, as far as I know.</p> 
                  <p>This is supremely expert, I wonder if anyone out there has succeeded<br> in doing so?</p> 
                  <p>I think we should make indexing a single byte[] as simple as indexing<br> a single String.</p> 
                  <p>This is a pre-cursor for issues like <a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/LUCENE-5596" title="Support for index/search large numeric field" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="LUCENE-5596"><del>LUCENE-5596</del></a> (encoding IPv6<br> address as byte<span class="error">[16]</span>) and <a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/LUCENE-5879" title="Add auto-prefix terms to block tree terms dict" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="LUCENE-5879"><del>LUCENE-5879</del></a> (encoding native numeric values<br> in their simple binary form).</p>
